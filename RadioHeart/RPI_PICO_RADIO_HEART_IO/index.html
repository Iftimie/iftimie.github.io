RadioHeart - RPI Pico Radio Heart IO

A pair of connected devices that signal presence through light and touch. This build combines a Raspberry Pi Pico, a LoRa radio module, and a simple touch interface to keep two people connected when they are within range.

Origin story

I got interested in radio and bought LoRa modules to test range. In early field tests I managed about 1 km without too many obstructions. That sparked the idea for a product with no moving parts. I paused further optimization to focus on learning how to sell and validate the idea first.

Product idea

The concept is a pair of hearts for couples that softly glow to show when the other is in range. A touch pattern on one device plays back on the other, giving a simple way to say hello.

Technical overview

  • Radio stack uses RadioLib with the LLCC68 module at 433 MHz.
  • Transmit config: TX power 10 dBm, bandwidth 250 kHz, SF7, CR5, CRC enabled, sync word 0x12.
  • Touch input records HIGH/LOW segments in 10 ms ticks and sends them with ACK retries.
  • Signal LED brightness maps RSSI/SNR into a combined quality score and fades over time.
  • Periodic PING packets keep presence updated even without touch input.
